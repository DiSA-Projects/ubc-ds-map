## Overview

UBC ARC Sockeye (“Sockeye”) is a high-performance computing platform available to UBC researchers in any discipline. 

With nearly 16,000 CPU cores, 200 GPUs, and 3 petabytes of storage capacity, Sockeye is designed to significantly increase UBC’s research computing capacity and supplement the national platform for digital research infrastructure (DRI) in order to meet the immediate needs of UBC researchers.

## Who Can Apply

All eligible UBC Researchers may request an allocation on the Sockeye platform. Allocations are awarded to one eligible UBC researcher, known as the Allocation Owner. The Allocation Owner may add any member of their research team, supporting staff, or collaborators to their allocation.  All allocation members require a UBC [Campus-Wide Login (CWL)](https://it.ubc.ca/services/accounts-passwords/campus-wide-login-cwl) and institutional email address to access the Sockeye platform.
